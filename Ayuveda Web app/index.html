Ayurvedic Routine

Morning Routine

Start your day the Ayurvedic way with these rejuvenating practices:

  • Wake up early: Rise before sunrise to align with natural rhythms.
  • Drink warm water: Flush out toxins and stimulate digestion.
  • Practice yoga or meditation: Set the tone for the day with mind-body exercises to promote balance and flexibility.
  • Ayurvedic self-massage (Abhyanga): Nourish your skin and improve circulation with warm oil massage before bathing.

Evening Routine

Wind down your day with these calming Ayurvedic rituals:

  • Have a light dinner: Opt for easily digestible foods to promote sound sleep.
  • Take a short walk: Aid digestion and clear the mind with a gentle stroll after dinner.
  • Wind down with herbal tea: Sip on soothing herbal infusions like chamomile or ashwagandha to relax the body and mind.
  • Pranayama (breathing exercises): Enhance respiratory health and calmness with deep breathing exercises before bed.
  • Journalling: Reflect on the day and set intentions for tomorrow to promote mental clarity and emotional balance.

History

Explore the journey and evolution of Ayurveda through centuries.

Ayurveda, originating from ancient India, is believed to be one of the oldest healing sciences. It has been passed down through generations, adapting and evolving over thousands of years. Originally documented in the Vedas, particularly the Atharva Veda, Ayurveda emphasizes a holistic approach to health, focusing on balance and harmony between body, mind, and spirit.

Over time, Ayurveda has integrated insights from various cultures and civilizations, incorporating knowledge of medicinal plants, dietary guidelines, yoga, meditation, and personalized therapies. Today, Ayurveda continues to thrive as a comprehensive system of natural health care, offering solutions for a wide range of health issues while promoting overall wellness and longevity.
